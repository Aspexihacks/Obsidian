# Defining the Haar System

$$\begin{aligned}
\phi_{1}&=1 \\
\phi_{2}&=\begin{cases}
1\quad,t\in[0, 1/2) \\
-1 \quad,t\in\left[ \frac{1}{2},1 \right]
\end{cases} \\
\phi_{3}&=\begin{cases}
1 \quad,t\in[0, \frac{1}{4}) \\
-1\quad ,t\in[\frac{1}{4}, \frac{1}{2}) \\
0\quad, \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}\\
\phi_{4}&=\begin{cases}
1 \quad,t\in[0, \frac{1}{4}) \\
-1\quad ,t\in[\frac{1}{4}, \frac{1}{2}) \\
0\quad, \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{aligned}$$
